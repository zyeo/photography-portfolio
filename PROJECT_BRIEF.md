# Photography Portfolio — Working Brief

## Core idea
A clean, image-first portfolio that feels calm, personal, and expandable.

## What it should do now
- Show strongest work quickly
- Make browsing photos feel effortless
- Let visitors understand the photographer behind the work
- Provide a simple path to contact

## Possible content structure
- Selected Work — the strongest, most intentional portfolio images
- Journal — looser daily photography, experiments, recent walks
- Places — photos grouped by location, likely Tokyo first
- About / Contact — short bio and a simple inquiry path

## Future-friendly directions
- Tokyo photography blog / field notes
- Service pages for freelance work
- Booking or inquiry workflow once there is a real offer to sell

## Open questions
- Should the site feel more editorial, cinematic, documentary, or minimalist?
- Is the photographer trying to attract clients, build an audience, or simply make a beautiful home for the work first?
- Are locations central to the identity of the work, or just one useful way to browse it?
